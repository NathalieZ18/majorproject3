UCF















My UCF Experience



























































First Impressions







Even though I didn't know much about UCF other than the fact that my sister
planned on attending and that their colors were basically the equivalent of a bee,
I always saw myself as a student there. Plus, bees are cool and
it wasn't too far from home, so I went.

Ironically, during my in-person orientation, I mistakenly visited a
different campus initially, thereby creating a less than ideal initial impression
of the esteemed university. Fortunately, I returned on time
and had the opportunity to observe the immaculate and aesthetically pleasing
environment of the downtown campus. The smaller size of the campus
stood out to me in comparison to its larger counterpart,
as it provided a more intimate and comfortable atmosphere.



















First Semester



The strategy was straightforward: submit an application, regularly attend lectures,
and avoid academic underperformance. Thus far, my initial term at the
University of Central Florida has been promising. My academic performance was
satisfactory as I received all A's, the coursework was not overly challenging, and
I was fortunate to have exceptional instructors. Despite encountering certain
obstacles, I experienced apprehension that my personal affairs might impede my focus
on academic pursuits. However, I successfully completed the first semester by overcoming its challenges.

Apart from my academic pursuits, I did not establish any new social connections. I attended a single class with a total of nine students, including myself. Due to the lecture-heavy nature of the session, I did not have the opportunity to engage in social activities. Furthermore, due to a lack of transportation and financial constraints, my ability to attend school clubs and events was severely limited. From a personal standpoint, I did not find the matter to be of much concern.







Take Two







Entering the subsequent and ongoing academic term, I was mindful that the
level of difficulty would increase. Is not that the fundamental purpose of attending school?
The instructional approach begins with rudimentary concepts and gradually advances
towards more complex material. The proceedings appeared to progress remarkably smoothly.
I achieved excellent grades, found the coursework to be reasonably manageable, and established
friendly connections with new acquaintances. Then, midterms arrived with an impact comparable
to that of a speeding truck traveling at a velocity of 100 miles per hour. In addition
to an increase in rigorous schoolwork, I experienced a significant personal transition that demanded
my complete focus, resulting in a detachment from my academic responsibilities. Regrettably,
I began to neglect assignments and submit them past their due dates,
which is a behavior that was previously uncharacteristic of me.















How About Now?



Despite the recent changes in my personal circumstances, I am trying to improve my academic standing by submitting upcoming assignments on time and submitting the majority of my overdue assignments. Although it is unfortunate that I was unable to perform to the best of my abilities, I take pride in my efforts to overcome the situation and persevere. A slight positive ending to this story, right?
